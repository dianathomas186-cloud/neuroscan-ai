GLIOMA SUMMARY:
Gliomas are primary brain tumors that originate in the glial cells (astrocytes, oligodendrocytes, and ependymal cells) which support and protect neurons. They are categorized into grades I through IV (with Grade IV being Glioblastoma).

COMMON SYMPTOMS:
- Headaches (often worse in the morning or during coughing)
- Seizures
- Gradual speech difficulties
- Cognitive decline, confusion, or memory issues
- Weakness or numbness on one side of the body

TREATMENT OPTIONS:
- Surgical resection: Maximize safe removal.
- Radiation therapy: Often combined with chemotherapy.
- Chemotherapy: Commonly Temozolomide (TMZ).
- Tumor Treating Fields (TTFields): For newly diagnosed glioblastomas.
