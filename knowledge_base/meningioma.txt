MENINGIOMA SUMMARY:
Meningiomas are tumors that arise from the meninges—the three layers of protective tissue membranes that surround the brain and spinal cord. They are most commonly benign (Grade I) and slow-growing.

COMMON SYMPTOMS:
- Vision changes (such as double vision or blurriness)
- Headaches (gradually worsening over time)
- Hearing loss or ringing in ears (tinnitus)
- Memory loss
- Loss of smell

TREATMENT OPTIONS:
- Observation (Active Surveillance): For small, slow-growing tumors causing no symptoms.
- Surgical resection: Primary treatment if symptoms develop or tumor grows.
- Radiation therapy: Recommended if the tumor is atypical, cancerous, or not fully resectable.
