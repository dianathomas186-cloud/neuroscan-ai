PITUITARY TUMOR SUMMARY:
Pituitary tumors (mostly adenomas) are abnormal growths that develop in the pituitary gland, a small pea-sized organ at the base of the brain responsible for regulating hormonal balance. Most are benign and slow-growing.

COMMON SYMPTOMS:
- Vision problems (specifically loss of peripheral vision due to optic chiasm pressure)
- Persistent headaches
- Hormonal imbalances (unexplained weight gain/loss, fatigue, menstrual changes, or erectile dysfunction)
- Excessive sweating

TREATMENT OPTIONS:
- Transsphenoidal surgery: Accessing and removing the tumor through the nasal cavity.
- Medications: Dopamine agonists to shrink prolactin-producing tumors.
- Radiation therapy: Stereotactic radiosurgery if surgery is not fully successful.
